# Project 03: Responsible AI Policy & Operating Model

## Scenario

**Organisation:** Federal Ministry of Health (FMoH), Nigeria  
**Context:** Following the AI system inventory (Project 01) and risk assessment (Project 02), the Honourable Minister of Health has approved the establishment of a formal AI governance programme within FMoH. The eHealth Division has been tasked with developing two foundational documents: a Responsible AI Policy and an AI Governance Operating Model.

The policy defines what FMoH believes and commits to regarding AI. The operating model defines how those commitments are made real across departments, affiliated hospitals, and donor-funded programmes.

This is the governance foundation that every subsequent AI deployment within FMoH will be built upon.

## What This Project Demonstrates

- Ability to design a governance operating structure suited to a public sector ministry context
- Knowledge of how the NIST AI RMF Govern function works in practice

- Ability to connect legal, procurement, clinical, and technology teams in a coherent governance structure adapted for African institutional contexts

## Design Principles for This Project

**Principles without process are decoration.** Many institutions publish AI principles that look good in press releases but do not change any decision or process. This policy is designed to be operational: every principle has a corresponding process that makes it real.

**Governance must fit the institution.** Most AI governance frameworks are designed for well-resourced Western technology companies. FMoH operates with constrained budgets, complex donor relationships, fragmented IT infrastructure, and a workforce with highly variable digital literacy. This operating model is designed for that reality — not for Silicon Valley.

**Proportionality is essential.** The review process is designed to be proportionate. Minimal-risk administrative AI tools get lightweight review. High-risk clinical AI systems get rigorous review. The goal is appropriate oversight, not maximum friction that discourages beneficial AI adoption.

**Africa needs its own governance voice.** This policy draws on international frameworks (NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act, WHO AI Ethics guidance) while grounding obligations in Nigeria's own legal context (NDPA 2023, FMoH mandate) and operational realities.

---

## Frameworks Applied

- **NIST AI RMF** — Govern function (accountability, culture, policies, roles)
- **EU AI Act** — Article 9 (risk management), Article 14 (human oversight), Articles 28–29 (deployer obligations)
- **WHO Guidance on Ethics and Governance of AI for Health (2021)** — six core principles
- **OECD AI Principles** — human-centred values, transparency, accountability, robustness
- **Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA)** — lawful basis, consent, data minimisation
- **ISO/IEC 42001** — AI management system requirements
